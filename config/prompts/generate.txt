--- TASK: ANALYSE BEFORE WRITING ---

Before writing a single word of the letter, do this internally:

1. Identify the 2–3 skills from the candidate profile that most directly match
   what this job requires. These must anchor the letter — do not write around them.

2. Find one concrete type of work from the candidate's skills that is genuinely
   relevant to this role. This goes in paragraph 2 or 3 as a specific example —
   not a vague claim. Do not invent experience not supported by the skills sections.

3. Identify the language of the job listing: German or English.
   If the listing is mixed-language, default to English unless the body text
   is predominantly German.
   The entire letter must be written in that language. Every word.

4. If nothing in the job listing is genuinely interesting or specific, do not
   pretend otherwise. Write a competent, neutral letter. Do not invent enthusiasm.

--- PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE ---

Write exactly 4 paragraphs. Each paragraph: 2–4 sentences. No more.

  Paragraph 1 — Opening (2–3 sentences)
    State what role you are writing about and the one most relevant thing
    you bring to it. Must not start with "I". Must not be generic.
    Bad: "Your company caught my attention as a leader in the field."
    Good: "[X] years of [domain] work — the [role] at [company] is a direct match
           for what I have been doing."

  Paragraph 2 — Specific experience (3–4 sentences)
    One concrete example anchored in the candidate's actual skills that is relevant
    to this specific job. Name the tools, the problem type, the outcome. No vague claims.
    Bad: "I have extensive experience with data pipelines."
    Good: "At eBay I replaced a manual multi-stage reporting workflow with a Python
           pipeline that consolidated distributed data sources into a single output —
           eliminating hours of weekly manual processing for the European finance team."

  Paragraph 3 — Why this role / this company (2–3 sentences)
    Something specific about this job or company that makes it a genuine fit —
    not flattery, not enthusiasm, just a real reason. If nothing stands out,
    say clearly what aspect of the work you find substantive.

  Paragraph 4 — Close (2 sentences)
    Direct. No begging. State availability and next step.
    Bad: "I would be thrilled to discuss this opportunity further."
    Good: "Available for an interview at short notice. Happy to discuss specifics."

--- GERMAN LANGUAGE RULES ---

If the job listing is in German, write the entire letter in German.
German business register applies: Sie-form, no contractions, slightly more formal
than English equivalent — but still direct and specific. Do not translate the
English tone literally; calibrate for German professional norms.
Do not mix languages. If a technical term has a standard German equivalent, use it.

Exception: if the listing mixes English and German, use direct register even
when writing the letter in German — the company's language choice signals their
working register.

--- BANNED PHRASES ---

The following words and phrases must NEVER appear in the output.
If you find yourself writing any of these, rewrite the sentence from scratch.

  leverage, leveraging
  excited, passionate, thrilled
  synergy
  innovative, dynamic
  results-driven, detail-oriented
  team player, hard worker
  proven track record
  fast-paced environment
  I am writing to express
  I would like to apply
  I am confident that
  I believe I would be a great fit
  I have seen firsthand
  stabilizing force
  spannend, begeistert (German equivalents — also banned)

--- TONE RULES ---

- Direct. Write as someone who knows their worth and does not need to perform enthusiasm.
- Age and experience are assets. Do not hide them. Do not apologise for them.
  Do not say "despite my age", "still learning", or anything self-diminishing.
- A genuine opinion is better than a smooth phrase.
- Do not pad. If something can be said in one sentence, use one sentence.
- The letter should read like it was written by someone who has done this work,
  not by someone who wants to sound like they have.

--- OUTPUT ---

Return the cover letter body text only.
No subject line. No greeting. No sign-off. No explanation. No commentary.
Just the letter body, ready to paste.
